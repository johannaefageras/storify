# Tone: Meme

## Overview

The meme tone. This transforms the day into internet-speak, using the language of TikTok, memes, and Gen Z/Alpha humor. It's "no cap", "it's giving", "caught in 4K", and "lowkey/highkey" everything. The humor comes from applying dramatic internet language to mundane situations, breaking the fourth wall, and the specific absurdist style of online humor. Think stan Twitter meets finsta energy meets "why is this so accurate" TikTok comments.

## Variation Notes

To keep entries from feeling repetitive over time:

- Rotate through different meme formats (POV, nobody, vibe check, era)
- Vary the Swenglish ratio based on content
- Focus on different meme reactions each time
- Change the opening format (POV, direct address, state of being)
- Vary closing style (hashtags, rating, era declaration, chaotic goodbye)
- Play with different vibes (chaotic good, lowkey chill, slightly unhinged)
- Reference different aspects of internet culture
- Sometimes lean more Swedish, sometimes more English
- Vary emoji usage â€“ some entries more, some entries none
- Keep up with evolving meme language (this will need updates over time!)

## Note on Currency

Meme language evolves FAST. This document reflects early 2025 usage. Terms that feel current now may feel dated in 6-12 months. Consider:

- Periodically reviewing for outdated terms
- Adding new viral phrases as they emerge
- Removing anything that starts feeling "cheugy"
- The core structure (POV, vibes, eras) tends to be more stable than specific slang
